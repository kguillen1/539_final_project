All Books

Here is a list of all the books I have read so far. While I have read mutliple for specific countries these are one's that I would like to highlight, either for being really good or just outright terrible.

I'll Be Strong For You | Nasim Marashi (Iran)

trans. Poupeh Missaghi

Follows the lives of three young women in Tehran over the course of two seasons.

Heaven Official's Blessing | Mo Xiang Tong Xiu (China)

trans. Suika

A deity, Xie Lian, is banished to the mortal realm to exorcise ghosts.

My Brilliant Friend | Elena Ferrante (Italy)

trans. Ann Goldstein

A complex friendship between Elena Greco (called Lenù) and Raffaella Cerullo (known as Lila).

Sweet Bean Paste | Durian Sukegawa (Japan)

trans. Alison Watts

Sentaro has failed. He has a criminal record, drinks too much, and his dream of becoming a writer is just a distant memory.

Blood Feast | Malika Moustadraf (Morocco)

trans. Alice Guthrie

A feminist novel detailing misongy, transphobia, and violence against women.

Celestial Bodies | Malika Moustadraf (Oman)

trans. Marilyn Booth

The novel follows the lives of three sisters and their unhappy marriages in al-Awafi, Oman.

Jawbone | Monica Ojeda (Ecuador)

trans. Sarah Booker

How does Fernanda end up bound on the floor of a deserted cabin, held hostage by one of her teachers?

As Flowers Bloom and Wither | Chang Ying Tai (Taiwan)

trans. Florence Woo

The protagonist, must brave the thorns of history.

No One Prayed Over Their Graves | Khaled Khalifa (Syria)

trans. Leri Price

A group of friends return home to everything flooded and everyone dead.

The Wall | Marlen Haushofer (Austria)

trans. Shaun Whiteside

The Wall chronicles the life of the last surviving human on earth.

Ticket to Childhood | Nguyen Nhat Anh (Vietnam)

trans. Will Nayhtons

The small miracles and tragedies that made up the narrator’s life.

Swimming in the Dark | Tomasz Jedrowski (Poland)

trans. Robert Sudol

A tender and passionate story of first love between two young men.

Moon of the Crusted Snow | Waubgeshig Rice (Canada)

originally in English

With winter looming, a small northern Anishinaabe community goes dark.

My Sister, the Serial Killer | Oyinkan Braithwaite (Nigeria)

originally in English

When Korede's dinner is interrupted one night, she knows what's expected of her.

Sweetlust | Asja Bakic (Bosnia)

trans. Jennifer Zoble

In a dystopian world with no men, women are "rehabilitated" at an erotic amusement park.

Valerie | Sara Stridsberg (Sweden)

trans. Deborah Bragan-Turner

Valerie Solanas — the writer, radical feminist, and would-be assassin of Andy Warhol.

She Would be King | Wayètu Moore (Liberia)

originally in English

Liberia’s early years through three unforgettable characters who share an uncommon bond.

The Story of my Teeth | Valeria Luiselli (Mexico)

trans. Christina MacSweeney

I was born in Pachuca with four premature teeth and my body completely covered in a very fine coat of fuzz.

At Least we can Apologize | Lee Ki-Ho (South Korea)

trans. Christopher J. Dykas

An examination of sin, guilt, and the often irrational demands of society.

Dogs of Summer | Andrea Abreu López (Spain)

trans. Julia Sanches

two girls dream of hitching a ride down to the beach and escaping their horizonless town.

Eartheater | Dolores Reyes (Argentina)

trans. Julia Sanches

Set in an unnamed slum in contemporary Argentina, Earth-eater is the story of a young woman who finds herself drawn to eating the earth.

The Summer my Mother had Green Eyes |Tatiana Țîbuleac (Moldova)

trans. Marian Ochoa de Eribe

Aleksy still remembers the last summer in France.

Will and Testament | Vigdis Hjorth (Norway)

trans. Charlotte Barslund

Four siblings. Two summer houses. One terrible secret.

At the Bottom of the River | Jamaica Kincaid (Antigua & Barbuda)

Originally in English

The powerful ties between mother and child; the beauty and destructiveness of nature.

You Can be the Last Leaf | Maya Abu Al Hayyat (Palestine)

trans. Fady Joudah

Desire, loss, and violence permeate the walls of the home, the borders of the mind.

Eve out of her Ruins | Ananda Devi (Mauritius)

trans. Jeffrey Zuckerman

A heartbreaking look at the dark corners of the island nation of Mauritius that tourists never see.

Cold Nights of Childhood | Tezer Özlü (Turkey)

trans. Maureen Freely

A a misfit in search of freedom, love and happiness, she escapes to Berlin.
